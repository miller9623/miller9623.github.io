What is College Mentors for Kids at Virginia Tech all about?

By being involved in College Mentors for Kids, each mentor is able to guide their men-tee, also known as their “little buddy”, to understand the importance of education by encouraging them to reach their full potential, and to show them the importance of giving back to their community. Almost everyone has had some kind of mentor in their life that made a difference. Whether it be a family member, coach, youth volunteer, teacher, etc. there was someone who made a permanent impression. College Mentors for Kids is a non-profit organization that provides you with the chance to be that role model. Being a mentor is a way for us college students to get connected with each other, as well as the community around us. While being a mentor you can learn about different resources offered on campus, responsibility and leadership needed to be a positive role model, and how to put Virginia Tech’s motto into action: Ut Prosim — that I may serve!

The Value of Being a Part of College Mentors for Kids!

As a mentor, I realized how much I had gotten out of College Mentors for Kids. I would tell my parents about the different activity days. That Ellie seemed to really enjoy the program and that she liked hanging out with me every week. I realized that College Mentors for Kids was not only impacting Ellie’s life, but my life as well. I recognized the value of this program; that College Mentors for Kids not only gets kids involved in school and education, but that it also creates meaningful relationships between the mentors and the little buddies. By being a part of College Mentors for Kids, I was more involved with things around campus. I knew that no matter how stressful my week was I always had something to look forward to that would brighten up my week. Not only did I have something exciting to do every week, but I became more dependable because I wanted to be there for my little buddy.

Looking back on my experience with College Mentors for Kids, I realized how much of an influence this organization had been. Who would have guessed that spending two hours a week with someone could turn into such a memorable part of my life. Being in my Junior year of college, when people ask me what are some of my favorite memories so far I usually say going square dancing — something that is completely out of character for me, but a great experience — or going to Virginia Tech football games and jumping to “Enter Sandman”, or even going hiking and taking pictures of some spectacular views with friends, but one thing that I will always remember is being a part of College Mentors for Kids! This experience has been an amazing one; an experience that has changed me for the better!
